A toxic train wreck in Ohioignited a dayslong inferno, spewed poisonous fumes into the air,killed thousands of fishand left residents wondering if it’s safe to live in East Palestine.
The fiery derailment in early February promptedfears of a catastrophic explosionofvinyl chloride– a highly flammable chemical linked to an increased risk of cancer. After a mandatory evacuation order, crews released vinyl chlorideinto a trench and burned it off– averting an explosion but spawning new health concerns.
Officials have said tests show the air and municipal water are safe and allowed residents to return home. But some have reported a variety of new health problems includingrashes, nausea,bloody nosesandtrouble breathing.
Train operator Norfolk Southern must handle andpay for all necessary cleanup, and the company has sent somehazardous waste out of state– fueling more questions about safety.
